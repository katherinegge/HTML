There are six positions on a Volleyball court, and each position is vital to the team's success. Each position is unique and requires a specialized skill set. Almost always, two positions (a front-row and a back-row) will act as substitues for each other in the rotation. For example, the Middle Blocker and Libero are a pair of substitutes. The Opposite Hitter and Defense Specialist are a pair; a final pair is the Setter and Opposite Hitter.
